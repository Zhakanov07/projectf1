Most Critical Risks

The highest-priority risks for the Grand Prix Motors project are:

  • R-01 — Car authentication failure (Medium probability × High impact). A single counterfeit component scandal could destroy the brand's reputation and result in legal action.
  • R-03 — Key team member departure (Medium × High). Losing the lead developer with undocumented knowledge would stall the website and catalog delivery.
  • R-02 — Website performance degradation (High × Medium). Downtime during a race-weekend traffic spike means lost sales at peak demand.
  • R-06 — Schedule overrun (High × Medium). Scope creep could delay the platform launch past the season opener, missing the primary marketing window.

Why These Risks Are Dangerous

  • Brand & Trust: F1 car buyers expect world-class authenticity. Any lapse in verification (R-01) directly undermines the core value proposition.
  • Revenue Impact: Website downtime (R-02) during high-traffic events and schedule delays (R-06) directly reduce revenue and market opportunity.
  • Knowledge Loss: The project relies on specialized F1 and web-tech expertise. Losing key people (R-03) without documentation creates a critical bottleneck.
  • Legal Exposure: Cross-border compliance issues (R-04) and payment fraud (R-05) carry financial and legal consequences that could threaten the entire business.

Key Mitigation Actions

  • Authentication Protocol: Introduce FIA-certified lab analysis as a mandatory second step before any listing (R-01).
  • Infrastructure Hardening: CDN edge caching, auto-scaling, and lazy-loaded 3D assets to handle traffic spikes (R-02).
  • Knowledge Management: Cross-training program and maintained technical wiki to eliminate single points of failure (R-03).
  • Agile Delivery: Fixed 2-week sprints with scope freezes to prevent schedule overrun (R-06).
  • Contingency Plans: Graceful degradation with cached data for API outages (R-07); manual order workflow as fallback.
